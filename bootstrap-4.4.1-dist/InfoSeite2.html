Cave Diving

General:
As their name suggests you have to dive in a cave. You dive up to 40 metres and you have to navigate in darkness, so you need a good sense of direction. There are three zones you can dive.
First Zone: Cavern In the first zone, you dive up to 20 metres. In this depth you still have natural light. There is a fix rope which leads you the way down. Second Zone: Cave You dive without natural light up to 30 metres. You explore some small caves. Third Zone: Full Cave This is the last Zone. There is no limit for the depth. Your expeditions can take several days.
Requirements:
  • Minimum age: 16
  • Diver license: Rescue Diver
  • Minimum 25 loged dives
  • Minimum 5 loged dives at night
  • Valid dive report
Risks:
Cave diving is 130 times more dangerous than normal diving. Statistics show there is a link between the experience of a diver and accidents. The highest risk is to panic in the dark.
Equipment:
  • bottle with compressed air
  • regulator for breath
  • Wetsuit
  • flashlight
  • helmet
